Overview

Modernizing a complex logistics operations platform

DrayEasy is a B2B platform used by freight dispatchers and logistics coordinators to manage drayage operations. The existing interface had grown organically over years — dense, inconsistent, and difficult for new users to learn.

I led a comprehensive UI redesign focused on visual clarity, information hierarchy, and workflow efficiency.

Research

Understanding power users in a high-stakes environment

Through contextual inquiry sessions with dispatchers, I discovered that users were developing elaborate workarounds — sticky notes, external spreadsheets, and color-coded tabs — to compensate for the platform's lack of visual organization.

Key pain points: information overload on primary screens, inconsistent interaction patterns across modules, and poor discoverability of critical actions during time-sensitive operations.

[ Insert research findings or affinity map here ]
Solution

Clarity through structure and visual hierarchy

I redesigned the core information architecture, introduced a consistent design system, and rebuilt the primary dashboard with progressive disclosure — showing critical information at a glance, with details available on demand.

Special attention was paid to preserving power-user efficiency: keyboard shortcuts, bulk actions, and quick filters were made more discoverable without cluttering the main interface.
