Overview

Streamlining job posting for enterprise HR teams

MokaHR is a leading HR SaaS platform in China, used by thousands of enterprise companies to manage their end-to-end recruitment process. I redesigned the job posting workflow — one of the most-used features in the platform — to reduce the time and effort required to go from job requisition to live posting.

[ Insert workflow diagram or overview here ]
Problem

A multi-step process with too many handoffs

The existing job posting flow required HR managers to navigate across multiple sections, repeat information, and wait for approvals without visibility into the process. Users were abandoning the flow mid-way and completing postings over multiple sessions.

Through user interviews and session recordings, I mapped the key drop-off points and identified where friction was highest.

[ Insert user journey map or pain points here ]
Solution

A unified, step-by-step posting experience

I redesigned the posting flow as a guided wizard with clear progress indication, smart defaults based on previous postings, and an integrated approval tracker. Recurring information was pre-filled from company templates, and the review step gave a live preview of how the job posting would appear to candidates.

The new design reduced the average number of steps from 11 to 6 while maintaining all required compliance fields.

Reflection

Designing for enterprise constraints

This project taught me how to balance user experience ideals with the reality of enterprise systems — compliance requirements, legacy data models, and multi-stakeholder approval chains. The biggest win wasn't just the redesigned UI, but the conversations it sparked internally about simplifying the underlying process itself.
